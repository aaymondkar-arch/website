Project 03

Building Performance

Building Performance & Design Integration · 2026 · Holyoke, MA

Designing with climate data, not against it.

A performance study of a residential courtyard development in Holyoke, Massachusetts - two L-shaped apartment buildings and a row of townhouses wrapping a shared courtyard and greenhouse. The work moves through four tasks: a climate analysis that turns psychrometric, radiation, and UTCI data into concrete design drivers; solar radiation studies that shape the massing, window-to-wall ratios, and a rooftop PV array; daylight analysis; and a shoebox energy study.

Each pass measurably improved the design. Average useful daylight illuminance rose from 14.3% to 80.6% and spatial daylight autonomy from 13.6% to 100%. Envelope iterations - revised facade geometry, a triple-insulated wall assembly with an aluminium rain screen, and double glazing - cut energy use intensity by 74%, served by a ground-source heat pump with dedicated outdoor air system.
