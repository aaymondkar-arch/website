A short introduction.

Trained as an architect, wired as an artist. I move between structure and sensation, drawing from the full spectrum of human creativity: the tension in a film frame, the weight of a typeface, the silence between notes, the story a photograph refuses to explain.

Architecture taught me that every constraint is a question, and every question has an answer worth finding. That instinct, to solve, to untangle, to make something work and mean something, travels with me into every field I touch.

For me, design is never just form following function. It's form following feeling, shaped by everything seen, heard, worn, and wondered about.

I build environments. But first, I pay attention.
